Introduction to Math Concepts

Seventh-grade math kicks off by mastering the full rational number system, teaching you how to confidently add, subtract, multiply, and divide negative numbers, fractions, and decimals. Once you have those number skills down, you jump into ratios and rates. You'll learn how to find unit rates (like miles per hour) and figure out if two quantities are changing at the exact same speed.

Next, you apply those ratios to everyday life, learning the exact math used at the grocery store or a restaurant to calculate tips, shopping sales, and sales tax. This is also where you scale down massive blueprints or maps to fit on a small page. After that, you'll dive into introductory algebra, learning how to balance and solve two-step expressions and equations to hunt down a missing variable ($x$).

Finally, you put your new algebra skills to work in geometry by solving for missing angles and using proportions to calculate the area, volume, and surface area of shapes like triangles and prisms. The year wraps up with statistics and probability, where you learn how to analyze data from groups of people, read statistical charts, and predict the exact mathematical chances of an event happening.
